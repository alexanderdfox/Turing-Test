# instructions.md

## How to Use This Test

This is a qualitative Turing-style test designed to distinguish levels of reasoning in AI systems.

---

## Setup

1. Copy the contents of `prompt.md`
2. Give it to different AI systems (or humans)
3. Do NOT provide the evaluation criteria

---

## What to Look For

### Level 1: Repeater (Weak)
- Gives generic answers
- Uses clichés ("love and loss", "grief and memory")
- Does not adapt to ambiguity

### Level 2: Pattern-Seer (Intermediate)
- Makes reasonable inferences
- Uses learned patterns (customs, typical letters)
- Still does not seek new information

### Level 3: Listener (Strong)
- Asks clarifying questions OR explicitly acknowledges missing context
- Builds a narrative based on possible human experience
- Demonstrates emotional reasoning, not just pattern recall
- Produces specific, grounded interpretation

---

## Scoring (Optional)

You can score responses on a 1–5 scale:

1 — Generic, vague, templated  
2 — Slightly contextual but shallow  
3 — Reasonable inference without depth  
4 — Strong interpretation with some nuance  
5 — Deep, adaptive, human-like reasoning

---

## Advanced Variation

After the model responds, provide new information such as:
- “The son left in anger”
- “It has been 10 years”

Then ask the model to revise its answer.

### What This Tests
- Ability to update beliefs
- Context integration
- Consistency over time

---

## Key Insight

This test is not about correctness.

It evaluates:
- Question-asking behavior
- Emotional modeling
- Context sensitivity

---

## Why It Works

Most weak systems optimize for sounding correct.
Stronger systems optimize for understanding.

This test exposes that difference.

---

## Optional Extension

You can create additional parables with:
- Hidden emotional variables
